# Bootstrapping, Moving to Portugal and Startup Distribution (Wannabe Entrepreneur Podcast)

Source:  
https://levels.io/wannabe-entrepreneur/

---

## Core Idea

In this interview, Pieter Levels shares his philosophy on bootstrapping startups, building independently, and distributing products through communities rather than relying on funding or paid ads.

He explains how solo founders can create meaningful businesses by building useful products, shipping quickly, and learning directly from market reactions.

---

## Why This Matters for Reddit Marketing

Pieter directly explains that every platform has different behavior rules.

What works on Instagram or X does **not** work on Reddit.

He specifically says Reddit is strongly anti-spam, meaning founders cannot simply copy-paste promotional content across platforms. Success on Reddit requires contribution and usefulness first.

---

## Reddit Strategy Insight

According to Pieter, the correct way to approach Reddit is:

- give value first  
- become a useful community member  
- contribute naturally  
- ask for feedback  
- avoid “I launched my startup” style promotion  
- respect platform culture

This makes Reddit more of a relationship platform than a broadcasting platform.

---

## Founder-Led Distribution Lesson

Pieter’s broader growth philosophy emphasizes:

- build fast  
- launch early  
- test real demand  
- learn from real users  
- use communities for traction

That aligns perfectly with how startup founders can use Reddit to validate ideas and gain early users.

---

## B2B SaaS Application

For B2B SaaS founders, this means Reddit can be used for:

- customer pain-point research  
- early feedback collection  
- positioning tests  
- trust building inside niche communities  
- soft-launching useful tools or insights

---

## Extracted Pattern

Reddit rewards founders who contribute before they promote.
