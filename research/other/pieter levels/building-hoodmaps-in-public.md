# Building Hoodmaps in Public: From First Line of Code to Frontpage of Reddit

Source:  
https://levels.io/hoodmaps/

---

## Core Idea

Pieter Levels documents how he built Hoodmaps publicly from scratch, then launched it successfully to the front page of Reddit, generating over 200,000 users in a single launch cycle.

This is a strong case study in community-led growth, founder-led distribution, and how Reddit can create massive awareness for new products.

---

## What is Hoodmaps?

Hoodmaps is a crowdsourced map that lets people label neighborhoods using categories such as:

- hipster  
- tourist  
- wealthy  
- student  
- business  
- suburban

The product solves a real traveler problem: understanding the personality of a city beyond tourist zones.

---

## Growth Strategy Used

Pieter did not rely on paid ads.

Instead, he used:

- building in public  
- livestreaming development  
- community feedback  
- iterative product updates  
- strategic multi-platform launching

He explicitly targeted Product Hunt, Hacker News, and Reddit as core launch channels.

---

## Why Reddit Worked

Reddit helped Hoodmaps spread because the product was:

- visual  
- fun  
- highly shareable  
- opinion-driven  
- community-friendly  
- useful enough to discuss

That combination fits Reddit culture extremely well.

---

## Launch Results

After launch:

- 200,000+ users  
- 500,000+ uses  
- nearly 30,000 people actively contributed  
- millions of map points created

This demonstrates how Reddit can drive both traffic and participation, not just passive clicks.

---

## B2B SaaS Lesson

Even though Hoodmaps is consumer-facing, the growth principles apply to B2B SaaS:

- Build products people naturally talk about  
- Launch where communities already gather  
- Use public feedback loops  
- Make the product interactive  
- Let users become contributors

---

## Extracted Pattern

Reddit rewards products that feel interesting, useful, and community-owned more than products that feel marketed.
