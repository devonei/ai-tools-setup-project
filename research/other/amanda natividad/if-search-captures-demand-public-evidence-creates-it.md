# If Search Captures Demand, Public Evidence Creates It

Source:
https://sparktoro.com/blog/if-search-captures-demand-public-evidence-creates-it/

---

## Core Idea

Search often receives credit for demand that was actually created elsewhere.

Buyers may discover, trust, and remember a brand through multiple touchpoints before ever searching for it.

These touchpoints include:
- LinkedIn posts
- Podcasts
- Newsletters
- Slack recommendations
- Reddit discussions
- AI-generated answers

---

## Key Insight

The channel that captures demand is not always the channel that created it.

Google Analytics may show search traffic, but awareness and trust are often formed on third-party platforms first.

---

## Why Reddit Matters for B2B SaaS

Amanda cites research showing Reddit increasingly appears before vendor websites in buyer journeys.

Reddit influences discovery through:
- category comparisons
- software recommendations
- user complaints
- peer trust signals
- long-tail problem searches

This means Reddit contributes to demand creation even when attribution misses it.

---

## Public Evidence Framework

Modern brand growth depends on the total public evidence surrounding a company.

This includes:

- Website content
- Reviews
- Forums
- Search snippets
- Brand mentions
- Reddit threads
- Pages AI tools can easily cite

---

## AI Search Relevance

If a brand's strongest proof points are not published publicly, AI systems may rely on weaker third-party evidence instead.

That makes Reddit and review platforms increasingly influential.

---

## B2B SaaS Application

For SaaS brands, Reddit should be treated as:

- reputation surface
- trust validator
- buyer research channel
- demand creation layer
- AI citation source

---

## Extracted Pattern

Modern growth path:

Awareness on communities → Trust via public evidence → Search capture → Conversion

---

## Final Principle

Search captures intent.

Public evidence creates it.
