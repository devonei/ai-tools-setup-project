## Core Idea

Most advertisers focus heavily on:

- Facebook
- Instagram
- TikTok
- YouTube

Neil argues this creates a blind spot.

Platforms like Reddit, Pinterest, Snap, and X are often ignored, which can create cheaper and less competitive opportunities.

---

## Why Reddit Matters

Reddit may not deliver the same raw volume as the largest platforms, but it can offer:

- lower ad costs
- niche targeting
- highly engaged communities
- less crowded auctions
- strong intent-based audiences

Neil frames Reddit as an undervalued paid acquisition channel.

---

## Strategic Insight

When competitors all buy attention in the same places, CPMs rise and efficiency drops.

Underserved platforms can become profitable because:

- fewer brands compete
- audiences are more specific
- costs can stay lower
- novelty can improve response rates

---

## B2B SaaS Application

For software companies, Reddit ads may work well for:

- niche products
- technical audiences
- founder tools
- developer software
- finance tools
- research-heavy buyers

Especially when users already gather in relevant subreddits.

---

## Practical Takeaway

Do not judge channels only by size.

Sometimes smaller platforms with sharper intent outperform larger noisy networks.

---

## Extracted Pattern

Ignored channels often become efficient channels.
